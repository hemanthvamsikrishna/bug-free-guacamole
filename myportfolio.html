My Startup Journey

🌱

Vaaraahi Farms: Growing Tech in Soil

In 2020, I launched Vaaraahi Farms, an AI-driven hydroponics system that integrated IoT sensors with automated growing environments. The vision was ambitious: democratize urban farming through intelligent automation.

We built a working prototype, integrated real-time sensor networks, and developed predictive models for crop yield optimization. But reality hit hard—customer acquisition costs outpaced revenue, and scaling hardware proved more capital-intensive than anticipated.

The lesson? Technology alone doesn't create value. Understanding market readiness, distribution channels, and unit economics matters just as much as the innovation itself.

🔍

Hive Quality Network: Distributed Intelligence

Learning from Vaaraahi, I pivoted to Hive Quality Network—a distributed defect detection platform for manufacturing. Computer vision meets edge computing, designed to scale across multiple production lines.

We achieved 98.7% accuracy on defect classification and reduced false positives by 60%. The tech worked brilliantly. But enterprise sales cycles are measured in quarters, not weeks, and we couldn't sustain runway.

Second failure, deeper lesson: Build for a market you can reach. The best product means nothing if you can't get it in front of the right buyers at the right time.

The Vision

I'm not here to build another SaaS tool or productivity app. I'm here to create the kind of technology that makes people say "How is this even possible?"

The idea keeping me up at night? It's bigger than a product—it's a paradigm shift. And when it launches, it won't just disrupt markets. It'll create new ones.
